Introduction

  • The Premier League, also known outside England as the English Premier League or the EPL, is an English Football League System which consists of 20 clubs. Each team faces all other 19 teams, on home and away, which results in a single team playing 38 matches per season. The season is held from August to May annually.

  • Broadcasted in 212 regions, to a staggering 643 Million residences and with a highly impressive potential television audience of 4.7 Billion people, the Premier League is the most watched sports league in the world. During the 2018-2019 season, the average attendance for matches was 38,000+. The Premier League also boasts a mind blowing aggregate match attendance of more than 14,508,980 people.

  • Since its inception in 1992, 49 clubs have competed in this League System. While 2 of them are Welsh clubs, the remaining 47 are English. So far, 7 teams have succeeded in securing victory at the Premier League.

History

  • During the mid-2000’s the Premier League saw the rise and dominance of the so-called “Top Four” clubs. These clubs were Arsenal, Chelsea, Liverpool, and Manchester United. These clubs qualified and secured victory excellently during the mentioned time period. Arsenal was even dubbed “The Invincibles” for their achievement of completing a Premier League without the loss of a single game. As of to date, this is the only time it has ever happened in a Premier League.

  • In 2009, a massive shift took place in the momentum of the “Top Four”, with the emergence of Tottenham Hotspur and Manchester City. The Top Four found their places being regularly taken by these two teams. As a result the “Top Four” quickly became the “Big Six”.
